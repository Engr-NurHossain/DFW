Recommendation for Location of Smoke and Heat Detectors
The major threat from fire in a family living unit is at night when everyone is asleep. The principal threat to persons in sleeping areas comes from fires in the reminder of the unit. Therefore, smoke alarm(s) should be located outside each sleeping area(s) on each level/floor.

ONIT Smart Home follows the recommendations contained in the U.S. National Fire Protection Association’s (NFPA) Standard #72 for the number and placement of smoke and heat detectors stated below:
  • Install a smoke detector inside every bedroom. A smoke alarm in the hallway outside the sleeping area may not wake up the sleeper if the door is closed.
  • Smoke detectors shall be installed outside of each separate sleeping area, in the immediate vicinity of the sleeping rooms.
  • Install a smoke detector at both ends of a hallway if the hallway is more than forty (40) feet (12 meters) long.
  • Smoke Detectors shall be installed on each additional story of the home.
  • Adequate notification devices required sounders shall be on each floor.
The selection and location of the minimum required smoke alarms does not provide adequate protection for the occupants from a fire starting in their bedrooms, nor do the required smoke alarms provide reliable early warning protection for those areas separated by a door from the areas protected by the required smoke alarms. For these reasons, it is recommended that the householder consider the use of additional smoke detectors for those areas for increased detection. The additional areas include: finished basement, dining room, and hallways not protected by the required smoke alarms. The installation of smoke detectors or alarms in kitchens, attics (finished or unfinished), or in garages is prohibited as these locations occasionally experience conditions which may result in improper operation and produce multiple false alarms. The installation of heat detectors and smoke detectors or alarms in detached buildings is prohibited.

Emergency Planning
Since an emergency is always unexpected, you should develop plans to help prepare for a variety of emergency situations. Periodically discuss and rehearse emergency plans with your family to include the following:
  • Understand how to use your security system.
  • Know the normal state of doors and windows: open, closed, or locked.

  • Crawl and hold your breath as much as possible to help reduce smoke inhalation during your escape.
  • Meet at a designated outdoor location. Emphasize that no one should return to the premises if there is a fire.
